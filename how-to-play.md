# How to Play Trumple

Trumple is a 4-player trick-taking card game played with a standard 52-card deck. It features unique bidding mechanics, dynamic trump selection, and a scoring system that rewards precision.

## Getting Started

1. **Create or join a lobby** — The host creates a lobby and gets a 6-character room code to share with friends
2. **Fill the table** — 4 players are needed. Add AI bots to fill any empty seats
3. **Play** — Each game consists of a bidding phase followed by 13 tricks

---

## The Cards

Trumple uses a standard 52-card deck. Each player is dealt 13 cards.

**Suit ranking** (low to high): Diamonds < Clubs < Hearts < Spades

**Card ranking** (low to high): 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10, Jack, Queen, King, Ace

---

## Phase 1: Bidding

Each player selects one or more cards from their hand as a bid. Your bid cards are revealed to all players, but they **stay in your hand** for trick play — they are not discarded.

**Bid values:** Number cards (2–10) are worth their face value. Aces are worth 1. Face cards (Jack, Queen, King) are worth 0.

Your bid total is the sum of your bid card values. This is the number of tricks you're aiming to win.

### Trump Selection

Your bid cards determine the trump suit for the game:

- **All bid cards are the same suit** — that suit becomes trump
- **Mixed suits** — no trump for this game

### Who Wins the Bid?

The player with the highest bid value wins the bid and leads the first trick. Ties are broken by:

1. Trump bid beats no-trump bid
2. Highest suit among bid cards
3. Fewest number of bid cards

### The 13-Rule

If all four players' bids add up to exactly 13, the bid winner gets to choose **UP** (+1 to all bids) or **DOWN** (-1 to all bids). This adds a twist — the bid winner can adjust everyone's target to gain an advantage.

---

## Phase 2: Trick Play

The game consists of exactly **13 tricks**. Every player plays all 13 of their cards.

### How a Trick Works

1. The lead player plays any card from their hand
2. Each other player must **follow suit** (play a card of the same suit) if they have one
3. If you don't have a card of the led suit, you may play any card
4. **Trump cards** played when you can't follow suit are placed **face-down** — other players won't see them until the trick is revealed

### Who Wins the Trick?

- If any trump cards were played: the **highest trump** wins
- If no trump cards were played: the **highest card of the led suit** wins

The trick winner leads the next trick.

---

## Scoring

After all 13 tricks are played, scores are calculated based on how close each player came to their bid:

| Result | Points |
|--------|--------|
| **Exact bid** (bid > 0) | +bid value |
| **Under bid** (bid > 0) | -1 per trick under |
| **Over bid** (bid > 0) | -2 per trick over |
| **Zero bid, won 0 tricks** | +5 |
| **Zero bid, won 1 trick** | -5 |
| **Zero bid, won 2+ tricks** | -5, then -2 per extra trick |

**Precision is key.** Hitting your bid exactly is the goal. Going over is punished more harshly than going under. A zero bid is a high-risk gamble — if you avoid winning any tricks you score +5, but even a single trick costs you -5.

---

## Multi-Game Sessions

After scoring, the host can choose **Next Game** or **End Game**. In a multi-game session, cumulative scores carry over across games. The player with the highest total score at the end wins the session.

---

## Tips for New Players

- **Start with moderate bids** — Bidding too high or too low is risky until you learn the flow
- **Pay attention to bid reveals** — Other players' bid cards tell you a lot about their hand and strategy
- **Trump is powerful** — Setting a trump suit gives you control, but reveals your strong suit to opponents
- **Zero bids are bold** — Winning zero tricks for +5 points is tough when opponents can force cards on you
- **Watch the led suit** — If you're void in a suit, you can play trump face-down for a surprise win
